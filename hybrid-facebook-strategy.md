# Hybrid Facebook Strategy: Personal Profile + Page
## Punta Cana Properties — Rent & Go PC

---

## THE TWO-CHANNEL SETUP

| Channel | Role | Strengths | Limitations |
|---------|------|-----------|-------------|
| **Personal Profile** | Primary — trust, relationships, organic reach | Already trusted in groups, DMs feel personal, high engagement | Can't run ads, no Pixel, no business tools |
| **Page: Rent & Go PC** | Technical — ads, tracking, business presence | Meta Pixel, paid ads, WhatsApp button, lead forms, analytics | Starts from zero, low organic reach, feels "corporate" |

**The core principle**: Your personal profile is the FACE. The page is the ENGINE. People buy from people, not pages. But the page gives you tools (ads, Pixel, retargeting) that the profile can't.

---

## 1. CONTENT SPLIT

### Personal Profile ONLY
These posts work because they come from a PERSON, not a brand:

- **Personal stories**: "I just showed a couple from NJ the apartment and their reaction was..." / "A client asked me why Punta Cana and not Cancun. Here's what I told them..."
- **Lifestyle photos/videos from YOUR life**: Morning coffee on the terrace, walking to the beach, dinner out in Bavaro. Caption: just living, not selling.
- **Opinion posts**: "Here's what I think people get wrong about buying in the DR..." — these spark debate and comments, which boosts reach.
- **Group replies and comments**: When someone in a group asks "Is it safe to buy in Punta Cana?" — YOU answer thoroughly. This is your #1 lead source. Be the helpful expert, not the guy with a listing.
- **DM conversations**: All serious buyer conversations happen in personal DMs. The page inbox is for cold leads from ads.
- **Personal milestones**: "Another closing day. Handed keys to a family from Boston. This is why I do this." (with permission)

### Page ONLY
These need business infrastructure:

- **Paid ads**: All campaigns (awareness, lead gen, retargeting) run from the page
- **Meta Pixel tracking**: Page owns the Pixel, which tracks website visitors for retargeting
- **WhatsApp click-to-chat ads**: Must come from a page
- **Lead form ads**: Facebook native lead forms require a page
- **Business information**: Address, hours, WhatsApp button, website link, reviews
- **Boosted posts**: If you want to amplify a post beyond organic reach
- **Video ads**: Professional property walkthroughs optimized for ad delivery

### BOTH (cross-post)
Some content works on both channels, posted natively to each (not shared):

- **Property tours**: Full video walkthrough — post natively to profile AND page. Different captions: personal on profile ("Let me show you what $315K gets you"), professional on page ("2BR at Cocotal Golf — virtual tour").
- **Market updates**: "Punta Cana tourism hit a new record this quarter. Here's what that means for property owners." Post to profile for engagement, post to page for SEO and ad amplification.
- **Educational carousels**: "5 things Americans don't know about buying in DR" — good content for both. On the profile it gets group shares. On the page it gets saved and can be boosted.
- **Testimonials**: Buyer stories work everywhere. Post the video/photo natively to both.

**IMPORTANT**: Never just "share" a page post to your profile. It kills reach. Always post natively to each channel with tailored captions.

### Posting Rules (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

1. **Maximum 1 listing post per group per week.** More than that = spam flags.
2. **For every listing post, make 3-5 value posts or comments first.** Answer questions, share tips, congratulate people, give honest advice. Build the bank before you withdraw.
3. **Never post the same text in multiple groups on the same day.** Facebook detects copy-paste across groups and throttles reach. Rotate templates.
4. **Always lead with a photo or video.** Text-only posts get buried in group feeds.
5. **Reply to EVERY comment on your posts** — even "nice" or a thumbs up emoji. It signals engagement to the algorithm and boosts visibility.
6. **Best posting times**: 7-9 PM EST (diaspora after work), 11 AM-1 PM EST (lunch scroll), Sunday mornings (planning mode).
7. **Never tag people without permission.** Never add people to groups without asking.

### The Value-First Content Mix for Groups

For every 1 listing/sales post, publish 4 value posts:

**Value Post Types**:
- **Answer questions**: "Someone asked about the buying process in DR. Here's my step-by-step breakdown..." (long, detailed, helpful)
- **Market insight**: "Just saw the latest tourism numbers for Punta Cana. Here's what it means for property investors..."
- **Comparison / myth-busting**: "A lot of people think you need $200K cash to buy in Punta Cana. Here's the reality..."
- **Life update / lifestyle**: "Saturday morning view from Cocotal. Still can't believe this is real life." (no sales pitch, just the photo)
- **Red flag warnings**: "5 things that should scare you away from a developer in DR. This is serious." (builds trust as honest advisor)
- **Congratulations / community**: Comment on other people's posts. Celebrate someone's move, offer advice on their question.

**Listing Post Formula** (when you DO post a property):
1. Open with a strong visual (best photo)
2. Lead with lifestyle or value, not specs: "What does $315K buy you in Punta Cana? More than you'd think."
3. Key specs in bullet points (not a wall of text)
4. Soft CTA: "DM me if you want to see more" — never "BUY NOW" or "DON'T MISS OUT"
5. Include site link at the end, not the beginning

### Avoiding Bans

- **Never use link shorteners** (bit.ly, etc.) — Facebook flags them as spam
- **Don't post your website link in the main text** of group posts — put it in the first comment instead. Facebook suppresses posts with external links.
- **If your post gets removed**, don't repost it. Message the admin and ask what went wrong. Adjust.
- **If you get restricted from posting**, stop ALL promotional activity for 2 weeks. Only comment helpfully on other people's posts.
- **Vary your post format**: text, photo, video, carousel, poll. Same format every time looks automated.
- **Never use engagement bait language**: "Comment YES if...", "Share this post", "Tag 3 friends" — Facebook penalizes this.

## 3. PAGE GROWTH PLAN

The page starts from zero. Here's how to build it up using your existing personal reach.

### Month 1: Foundation (0-200 followers)

- **Invite all personal friends** who might be interested (not everyone — targeted invites)
- **Post 3-5x/week on the page**: Property tours, lifestyle content, market info. Build a content library.
- **Share page posts TO YOUR STORY** (not your timeline — story shares don't kill reach as badly)
- **When you post in groups from your personal profile**, have a subtle mention: "I share more properties and market updates on my page [name] if you want to follow along"
- **Pin a post on your personal profile**: "If you're interested in Punta Cana real estate, I post detailed market analysis and property tours on my page: [link]"
- **Set up the page fully**: Profile photo (your face, not a logo), cover photo (Cocotal terrace view), WhatsApp button, website link, about section with your story

### Month 2: Traction (200-500 followers)

- **Run a small "Page Likes" campaign**: $50-100, targeting the same audiences from the ad strategy. Gets you initial social proof.
- **Cross-promote with content**: Post a carousel on your profile → "Full breakdown with all the numbers is on my page [link]." Drive traffic.
- **Start a weekly Facebook Live on the page**: Property tours, Q&A sessions, market updates. Lives get massive organic reach on pages.
- **Engage with followers**: Reply to every comment, react to shares, message people who engage regularly.

### Month 3: Scale (500-1,000+ followers)

- **Launch retargeting ads** via the page (Pixel should have data by now)
- **Create a lookalike audience** from page engagers and website visitors
- **Start running lead gen ads** with the page as the face
- **The page is now credible enough** that when someone Googles "Rent & Go PC" or sees an ad, the page looks legit with followers, content, and reviews
- **Ask satisfied clients to leave a review** on the page — this is GOLD for credibility

### The Bridge Move
When someone in a group DMs you on your personal profile and becomes a serious lead, at some point send them to the page: "I'll send you the property link from my business page so you have all the details in one place." This subtly introduces them to the page and increases its follower count organically.

## 4. PAID ADS VIA PAGE

### Pixel Setup
- Install Meta Pixel on all pages of rentandgopc.com (all language versions)
- Set up standard events: `PageView`, `ViewContent` (property page), `Lead` (WhatsApp click or form submit), `Contact`
- Let the Pixel collect data for 2-4 weeks before running retargeting

### Campaign Structure

**Campaign 1: Awareness (Video Views)**
- Budget: 40% of ad spend
- Content: Property walkthrough videos, lifestyle reels
- Audience: Interest-based targeting per segment (USA, Diaspora, Quebec, LatAm, Russian)
- Objective: ThruPlay (15-second video views)
- Purpose: Fill the top of funnel + build custom audiences of video viewers

**Campaign 2: Retargeting Website Visitors**
- Budget: 25% of ad spend
- Audience: Custom audience — anyone who visited the property page but didn't click WhatsApp
- Content: Single image ad with price, key feature, and WhatsApp CTA
- Objective: Conversions (WhatsApp click)
- Note: This won't have volume until the Pixel has been collecting for 4+ weeks

**Campaign 3: Retargeting Video Viewers**
- Budget: 15% of ad spend
- Audience: People who watched 50%+ of awareness videos
- Content: Lead magnet ("Download the Punta Cana Buyer's Guide") or direct WhatsApp CTA
- Objective: Lead generation

**Campaign 4: Lookalike Audiences**
- Budget: 20% of ad spend
- Source: 1% lookalike from website visitors, page engagers, or lead form submitters
- Content: Best-performing awareness creative
- Objective: ThruPlay or Lead generation
- Note: Need 100+ source audience before this works well. Start in month 2-3.

### Budget Recommendations
- Month 1: $300-500 (awareness only, build Pixel data)
- Month 2: $500-800 (add retargeting)
- Month 3+: $1,000-2,500 (full funnel with lookalikes)

## 8. DO'S AND DON'TS

### DO's

- **Be the helpful expert first, the salesman never.** If someone asks "Is Punta Cana safe?" — write a 300-word genuine answer. Don't end it with "and I have a listing."
- **Post consistently.** Disappearing for 2 weeks kills your momentum. The algorithm rewards consistency.
- **Respond to DMs within 2 hours** during business hours. Speed = seriousness. If you can't respond fully, at least acknowledge: "Hey! Got your message. Let me put together the info and get back to you today."
- **Use voice notes in WhatsApp.** It builds connection way faster than text. Hearing your voice = trust.
- **Film yourself on-location.** A selfie-style video from the Cocotal terrace beats a stock photo every time.
- **Share the REAL numbers.** People can smell inflated ROI projections. Be honest about costs, occupancy dips, management fees. Honesty is your competitive advantage.
- **Build relationships with group admins.** They control who stays and who gets banned. A good relationship = they'll let your posts stay up.
- **Track every lead in a spreadsheet or CRM.** Name, source (which group/ad), date, status, follow-up date. Without this, leads slip through the cracks.
- **Follow up 3 times.** Most leads go cold because the agent gave up after 1 message. Follow up at 3 days, 1 week, and 2 weeks. After that, add to a monthly newsletter/broadcast.
- **Ask for reviews and testimonials.** After every closing, ask the buyer to leave a review on your page and record a 30-second video. This is your most valuable content.

### DON'TS

- **Don't spam groups.** One bad impression = permanent ban. You lose access to thousands of potential leads.
- **Don't copy-paste the same post across 10 groups in one day.** Facebook flags this as spam. Max 2-3 groups per day, different text each time.
- **Don't post links in the main body of group posts.** External links kill organic reach. Put the link in the FIRST COMMENT.
- **Don't use engagement bait.** "Comment YES if you want details" / "Share this to win" — Facebook penalizes this and it looks desperate.
- **Don't argue in comments.** If someone is negative or trolling, respond once with facts, then disengage. Never get into a back-and-forth. Others are watching and judging your professionalism.
- **Don't hard-sell in DMs.** When someone messages you, LISTEN first. Ask what they're looking for, what their timeline is, what their budget is. Match their needs before pitching anything.
- **Don't share page posts to your personal timeline.** It kills reach on both. Post natively to each channel.
- **Don't run ads before your page has content.** If someone clicks your ad and sees a page with 3 posts and 12 followers, they bounce. Build the page for 2-4 weeks first.
- **Don't use "limited time offer" or fake urgency.** These are not Shopify products. Real estate buyers see through this instantly. Real urgency (last unit with this view, price increase at delivery) is fine — manufactured urgency is not.
- **Don't neglect the page because the profile works better.** The page is a long-term investment. It compounds over time as Pixel data grows, reviews accumulate, and ad audiences get smarter.
- **Don't mix personal life drama with real estate content.** Your profile is your brand. Keep controversial opinions, political posts, and personal venting off the timeline (or on restricted lists).
